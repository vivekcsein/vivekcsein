# AR User Instruction Guidelines

## Overview

One of the biggest reasons users abandon an AR experience is because they don't know what to do next.

Every AR experience should guide the user with clear, contextual instructions that appear only when needed. Instructions should be short (2–8 words), easy to understand, and disappear automatically once the user performs the required action.

Good onboarding can improve completion rates, engagement, and overall user satisfaction.

---

# Instruction Design Principles

## Keep Instructions Short

✅ Good

- Tap to Start
- Tap to Place
- Open Your Mouth
- Smile
- Pinch to Zoom

❌ Bad

- Please tap anywhere on your screen to begin the augmented reality experience.

---

## One Action at a Time

Never overload users with multiple instructions simultaneously.

Good Flow

```
Tap to Start
        ↓
Move your phone
        ↓
Surface Found
        ↓
Tap to Place
        ↓
Pinch to Resize
        ↓
Rotate to Inspect
```

---

## Use Visual Hints

Always combine text with visual indicators.

Examples

- Animated Finger
- Pulsing Circle
- Arrow
- Glow Effect
- Highlighted Button
- Loading Ring

---

## Auto Hide Instructions

Instructions should disappear immediately after completion.

Example

```
Tap to Place
↓
(User taps)
↓
Instruction disappears
↓
Next instruction appears
```

---

## Repeat When Necessary

If no interaction occurs after several seconds, gently remind the user.

Example

```
Still looking?

Move your phone slowly.
```

# Best Practices

- Show only one instruction at a time.
- Keep text under eight words whenever possible.
- Pair text with simple animations or icons.
- Remove completed instructions immediately.
- Repeat hints only after a short period of inactivity.
- Position instructions away from important AR content.
- Use high-contrast text for readability in all environments.
- Avoid technical terminology; use action-oriented language.
- Adapt instructions to the current stage of the experience.
- Always provide recovery guidance when tracking is lost.

# Goal

The objective of these instructions is to make every AR experience intuitive, reduce user confusion, and maximize engagement. By presenting concise, contextual guidance at each stage, users can seamlessly progress from launching the experience to exploring its interactive features with confidence.
